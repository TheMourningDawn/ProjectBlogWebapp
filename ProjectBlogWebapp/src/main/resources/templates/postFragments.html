I've found myself enamored by all the cool things Adafruit has in their learning section. I wanted to make so many of them. With things like {insert some links} it was tough to not go with something really crazy. Since I still had work to do on the totem , and I wanted something wearable, I decided to try out a pair of of the ever so overdone cliche popular 'steampunk' goggles. They were even so loved on Adafruit's site that they started selling kits with everything* necessary. No sense in arguing with the masses, right?

Some tiny portion of the reason I wanted to try the goggles was because all the components were so small. So far I'd mostly only done reasonable easy solders that weren't too close together. I'd also never had the pleasure of having to fit a bunch of stuff in a small area. Not to mention my curiousity about how realistic it would be to keep 32 LEDs powered.

* Only includes the most basic components and a pair of costume goggles. Great if you're a beginner and still learning.
